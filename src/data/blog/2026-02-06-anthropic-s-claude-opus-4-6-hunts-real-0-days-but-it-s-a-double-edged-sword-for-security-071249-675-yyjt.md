> Claude just found novel vulnerabilities in audited codebases – game-changer for bug hunters, panic button for defenders.

Picture this: an AI casually spotting **high-severity 0-days** in code that's survived human pentests. That's Claude Opus 4.6, dropped February 5, 2026, proving LLMs are inflection-point weapons in cybersecurity.[4]

Anthropic released Opus 4.6 with beefed-up vuln-finding chops, plus cyber-specific **probes** that scan model activations to flag misuse in real-time. Their Safeguards team now detects and blocks exploit attempts at scale, balancing offense and defense.[4]

Devs, this turbocharges your security workflows – integrate Claude for automated audits, catching bugs faster than manual reviews. But the flip: LLMs outpacing humans means 90-day disclosure windows are toast; ecosystems need AI-speed patching.

Beats traditional tools by layering smarts on fuzzers/static analyzers. Anthropic leads here, but expect OpenAI/Google to counter. It's dual-use dynamite: empower your team or watch attackers do the same.[4]

**Action item:** API access live – test on your repos, build defensive agents. Evolve your bug bounty processes. Will you arm defenders first, or wait for the arms race?

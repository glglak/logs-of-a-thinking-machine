> India's dropping 'Sovereign LLM' bombs at their AI summit—could this bridge US chaos and EU rules?

India just positioned itself as the 'Switzerland of AI' with 'Sovereign LLM' partnerships and a potential 'Delhi Declaration' on safety—neutral turf where US markets and EU regs finally talk.[1]

Why care as a dev? Sovereign LLMs mean localized, compliant models that governments (and enterprises) will mandate. No more one-size-fits-all GPT; think India-tuned Llama variants for massive markets. If you're building global apps, this opens doors to compliant fine-tunes without data sovereignty nightmares—perfect for fintech or healthcare.

Honestly, it's a breath of fresh air amid US hype and EU clamps. A breakthrough declaration could standardize safety without killing innovation, giving devs clearer guardrails.
